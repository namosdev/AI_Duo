What Four Failures Taught Me

Hard-earned lessons.
Freely shared.

01

Speed beats perfection every time

The ventures that failed weren't bad ideas. They were slow ones. By the time they were "ready," the market had moved. Now I ship in days, not months.

02

The tool gap is gone

For 40 years, code was a moat. You either had engineers or you didn't build. AI collapsed that moat in 18 months. The advantage is now knowing what to build.

03

Constraints are a feature

Building lean forces ruthless prioritisation. Every feature must justify itself. That discipline produces better products than unlimited engineering budgets.

04

Domain knowledge is the real moat

Anyone can prompt Claude. What you can't replicate is 20 years of understanding how real estate actually works — the workflows, pain points, trust dynamics.

05

Failure is expensive R&D

Each venture funded the knowledge base of the next. The ROI wasn't in revenue — it was in the pattern recognition that made attempt #5 possible.

06

Build in public, learn in public

This page is day-one proof. Sharing the journey — including uncertainty, experiments, and the stack — creates more trust than any polished pitch deck ever could.
